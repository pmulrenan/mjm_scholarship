About Michael

A story rooted in hard work, humility, and service.

Michael Mulrenan came from humble beginnings. His dad passed away at an early age, leaving his mom to raise nine kids on her own. Although they didn't have much, they had each other and a strong work ethic. That combination gave them the confidence they needed to pursue their dreams.

Michael's dream was to go to college, as he felt a postsecondary education was the key to new opportunities. Soon after graduating from Boston University with a master's degree in physical therapy, he opened an outpatient clinic specializing in orthopedics and sports medicine. He proved to be a successful entrepreneur when one clinic multiplied into forty-four over time.

Even with all this success, he never forgot where he came from and who helped him along the way. This humility prompted him to give back to his community whenever he could. In particular, he dedicated a lot of time to the Woburn-Stoneham Elks Lodge where he'd been a proud member for nearly thirty years.

In addition to posthumously receiving the Elks Distinguished Citizenship Award, Michael served as Trustee on the Board of Directors, initiated the 908 Cigar Room, and planned and managed the renovations of the Elks building including the extensive handicap project.
